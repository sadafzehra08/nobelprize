Welcome to Nobel Prize

The Nobel Prizes are prestigious international awards established by Alfred Nobel, a Swedish chemist and inventor, to honor significant contributions to humanity in the fields of Physics, Chemistry, Medicine, Literature, and Peace, with the Economic Sciences prize added later. First awarded in 1901, these prizes are managed by various institutions, including the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences and the Norwegian Nobel Committee, with ceremonies held annually on December 10, the anniversary of Nobel's death. Laureates receive a medal, diploma, and monetary reward in recognition of their groundbreaking achievements, with the Peace Prize awarded in Oslo, Norway, and the others in Stockholm, Sweden. Here are the winners of Nobel Prize in 2024

John J. Hopfield and Geoffrey E. Hinton :: Recognized for foundational discoveries and inventions that enable machine learning with artificial neural networks.

David Baker, Demis Hassabis, and John Jumper :: Honored for advancements in protein design using artificial intelligence, facilitating significant scientific breakthroughs.

Victor Ambros and Gary Ruvkun :: Awarded for the discovery of microRNA and its role in post-transcriptional gene regulation.

Han Kang ::Recognized for her intense poetic prose that confronts historical traumas and exposes the fragility of human life.

Nihon Hidankyo :: The Japanese organization of atomic bomb survivors honored for its efforts to achieve a world free of nuclear weapons and for demonstrating through witness testimony that nuclear weapons must never be used again.

Daron Acemoglu, Simon Johnson, and James A. Robinson ::Awarded for their studies on how institutions are formed and affect prosperity.

Nobel Prize FAQs

Explore Answers to Common Questions About the Nobel Prize

Who was Alfred Nobel, and why did he create the Nobel Prizes?

Alfred Nobel was a Swedish chemist, engineer, and inventor of dynamite. He established the Nobel Prizes in his will to recognize outstanding contributions to humanity in various fields.

How are Nobel laureates selected?

Each category has its own selection committee that evaluates nominations and selects laureates based on merit and contributions to their field.

What is the Nobel Peace Prize, and how is it unique?

The Nobel Peace Prize is awarded to individuals or organizations that have made significant contributions to peace. Unlike other prizes, it is awarded by the Norwegian Nobel Committee.

Can organizations win a Nobel Prize?

Yes, organizations can win the Nobel Peace Prize. Examples include the United Nations and the International Red Cross.

Who was the youngest Nobel Prize laureate?

Malala Yousafzai became the youngest laureate at the age of 17 when she won the Nobel Peace Prize in 2014.

What happens during the Nobel Prize ceremony?

The Nobel Prize ceremony is held annually in Stockholm, Sweden (except for the Peace Prize, awarded in Oslo, Norway). Laureates receive a medal, diploma, and monetary award.

Who established the Nobel Prizes?

The Nobel Prizes were established by Alfred Nobel, a Swedish chemist, engineer, and inventor of dynamite, through his will in 1895.

What are the categories of the Nobel Prizes?

The Nobel Prizes are awarded in six categories: Physics, Chemistry, Medicine, Literature, Peace, and Economic Sciences. The Prize in Economic Sciences was added in 1968 by the Sveriges Riksbank.

Who selects the Nobel Prize winners?

Each Nobel Prize has its own selection committee.
For example:
1:The Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences awards the prizes in Physics, Chemistry, and Economic Sciences.
2:The Karolinska Institute awards the prize in Medicine.
3:The Swedish Academy awards the prize in Literature. The Norwegian Nobel Committee awards the Peace Prize.

Can Nobel Prizes be awarded posthumously?

Nobel Prizes cannot be awarded posthumously unless the laureate passes away after being announced as a winner.

Why is the Peace Prize awarded in Norway?

Alfred Nobel specified in his will that the Peace Prize should be awarded by a Norwegian committee, as Sweden and Norway were in a union at the time, and he believed Norway had a tradition of peace.

What do Nobel Prize winners receive?

Winners receive: A medal. A diploma. A monetary award, which varies depending on the income generated by the Nobel Foundation's investments.
